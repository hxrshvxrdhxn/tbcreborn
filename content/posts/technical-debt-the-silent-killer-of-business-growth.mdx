Technical debt refers to the long-term cost of using temporary or suboptimal technology solutions instead of implementing scalable systems.

While technical debt may initially accelerate development or reduce costs, it often creates significant operational challenges later.

Common examples of technical debt include:

- Legacy systems that cannot integrate with modern tools
- Poorly structured databases
- Outdated software infrastructure
- Workarounds that replace proper system architecture

Technical consultants help businesses identify and reduce technical debt through structured modernization strategies.

Reducing technical debt allows companies to improve system reliability, security, and scalability.
